# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: Romanian cat owners who care about animal welfare and natural materials — the kind of buyer who wants a scratching tower built from real solid wood, without chemicals or adhesives, and who's drawn to the fact that a purchase does something beyond their own home. Secondary: potential collaborators reached through the Colaborari page — influencers with animal-loving audiences, companies interested in co-branded pieces, and shelters/NGOs looking for visibility and support. These two audiences read different pages with different goals; both deserve first-class treatment.

## Product Purpose

NEBI designs and sells modular cat towers made from solid fir wood, assembled without adhesives or chemical treatments, using natural wood joinery so pieces can be added over time like a construction set. The brand exists to fund a cause: 30% of profit goes to animal shelters, and every campaign doubles as an adoption-awareness effort for cats without homes. The project is currently pre-launch — the configurator and pricing already work end-to-end, but production/fulfillment isn't live and the real product photography hasn't been shot yet. Success right now means visitors understanding and spreading the mission; driving hard sales through the configurator is the secondary, not the primary, job of the site.

## Positioning

The only cat tower you build like LEGO from solid, chemical-free fir wood — and 30% of every purchase funds real shelter cats.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: understand the mission and share it (word-of-mouth ahead of full launch).
- Secondary CTA: try the configurator and see a real price for a real tower.
- Belief ladder: store-bought scratchers and furniture fall short of what a cat actually needs → NEBI's solid-wood, chemical-free, hand-joined build is genuinely better and can be trusted → buying or sharing funds real impact for shelter cats, not just marketing talk.
- Proof on hand: shelter and NGO relationships are in progress but not yet formalized or public; a real built prototype exists, though it isn't photographed for the site yet. Until then, the mission statement and configurator's real pricing are the strongest available proof — avoid manufacturing false proof (stock photography standing in for the prototype, invented testimonials) ahead of having the real thing.

## Brand Personality

Warm, friendly, and playful, with genuine empathy for animals — this is stated directly in NEBI's own brand guidelines (Colaborari page) and should be treated as settled, not reinterpreted. The pixel-art aesthetic is core identity, not decoration: it signals a maker's, nerdy-but-caring sensibility rather than a polished corporate pet brand. Emojis are part of the voice, used with moderation.

## Anti-references

Sterile, catalog-like corporate e-commerce — anything that reads cold or big-box-furniture-retailer rather than made-with-care. Also avoid the two adjacent traps this brand could slide into: generic pastel pet-startup sameness (soft gradients, blob illustrations) and overly childish cartoon-mascot pet branding — NEBI's playfulness is pixel-art-deliberate, not cutesy.

## Design Principles

Mission before funnel: every page should make the shelter impact feel real before it asks for money or an email, matching the belief ladder above. Craftsmanship as trust signal: the modular, chemical-free, hand-assembled construction should read as evidence the brand is legitimate, not just a bullet point. Playful but not childish: pixel-art and warmth carry the personality without tipping into a cartoon mascot brand. Honest about the pre-launch state: never simulate availability or proof that doesn't exist yet — no stock photography or invented testimonials standing in for the real prototype. Partners are a first-class audience: the collaboration surfaces for shelters, influencers, and brands deserve the same craft as the shopper-facing pages, since they're a primary channel for the mission right now.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target requested — baseline good practice is the bar: solid color contrast, full keyboard navigation, and respecting `prefers-reduced-motion` (relevant given the site's existing pixel-hop and float animations).
